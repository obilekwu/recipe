Egusi Soup with Pounded Yam

A classic Nigerian dish that combines rich, hearty flavors with the perfect starchy companion.

Recipe Information

Prep time: 25 minutes

Cook time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 1 smoked turkey (cut into pieces)
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 hot pepper
  • 1 medium onion (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp locust beans
  • 2 tbsp ground crayfish
  • 2 tbsp palm oil
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp garlic & ginger paste
  • 2 cups spinach (chopped)
  • Salt and seasoning cubes
  • Optional: mushrooms
  • Yam (for pounded yam)

Instructions

  1. Mix ground egusi with water to form a thick paste.
  2. Boil smoked turkey in water for 45 minutes, reserve stock.
  3. Sauté mushrooms in vegetable oil (if using), season, set aside.
  4. Blend peppers (and tomato if using) until smooth.
  5. Heat palm oil, sauté onions for 3 minutes.
  6. Add locust beans, garlic & ginger paste, and crayfish. Cook 5 mins.
  7. Add blended pepper, cook until reduced, stirring.
  8. Add turkey stock and simmer.
  9. Add spoonfuls of egusi paste, cover, cook 20–30 mins.
  10. Add turkey, mushrooms, and spinach. Simmer 5 mins.
  11. Season to taste. Serve hot with pounded yam.
  12. Pounded yam: boil yam cubes until soft, mash with reserved water, shape into balls.

Tips & Notes

Tip: Don’t over-stir the egusi — keeps the texture chunky and delicious!
